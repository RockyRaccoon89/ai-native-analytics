# Executive summary
## What is book about?
In the world of software development, we are facing a major change in how we build. LLM are naturaly great at programming since software languages have defined rules and at lot of availble documentation. With the avenue of agent the ai models are more than just easy autocomplete or a faster way to get stack-overflow snipet of code. They now have the hability to plan and build coherent code.
But they don't nearly ask enough questions, have no business intuition and have a limited capacity to hold long and complex context. This is an important limitation in software developement but it is especially true in the sphere of analytics. Analytics is all about how people thinks, what is strategic for the business, how to allow smart but bound to humain capacity people to understand extremely complex situation and increase probably of a good decision. 

**Push to the edges**
An oversimplify version of the analytics developpement process start with taking business requirements then building the proper data solution and finally building the humain interface.

Requirements gathering -> data solution building -> humain interface building

The middle part, "bulding the proper data solution", can be increasingly automated by ai agents with a major caviat. Ai agents must be properly feeded with information and directed correctly to make this data avaiable correctly. To adjust to this new reality, the analytics practitionners must refocus their energy towards the edges of the process. On the left side of the flow, it means investing more time understanding the business context and documenting it in a way the humain and artificial intelligences can comprehent and leverage. On the right side, the practionners must connect more deeply with the end customer to help them make better decision but also improve on their processes.
This book is about helping the analytics practitionners migrating their processes, tools, skill sets and documentation towards an environment where humain and machines does what they do best. Humains are best at connecting with each others, at moral jugement, imagining something new, strategic thinking and distilling what is important from the noise. Machines are best at managing minute details, processing vast amount of data, following rules and structure. 

**Dangers we must avoid**
The age of ai forces everyone to rethink everyone roles and it is critical that leaders understand quickly that the solution is not to replace humains with ai but to rethink what we should expect from people.
Also, simply increasing the quantity of output is another great danger. We must use this new capacity to invest in better analytics, not just more outputs. The simple action of "puking" more will have the inverst effect that expect, the customers will get quickly dull of "slop analytics" and will go back to their excel files that have all the sins of a bad systems but at least is connected to their needs.
